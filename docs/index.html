Common questions

Things people ask before getting started.

If your question isn't here, write to bart@msg2ai.xyz. Real human, fast reply.

Do I need to know how to code?
No. The browser path (Option 1 in "Get started") is just copy-paste into Claude.ai. The whole reason these are "skills" rather than software is that the work happens in plain English — yours, going in, and Claude's, coming back.
Will Claude make things up about my event?
Claude will produce a plausible draft from whatever context you give it. If you don't tell it the event is October 12, it will pick a date or ask. The skills are written to ask for the missing pieces first — event date, scale, audience — before producing output. Treat the first response as a strong first draft, not a final answer.
Is my conference data safe?
Your conversations stay in your Claude account. The skills don't send anything anywhere on their own — the only data Claude sees is what you paste or what's reachable through the integrations you choose to connect. If you want to keep things hyper-private, run them in a Claude.ai Project and skip the connectors entirely.
What does it cost?
The skills are free, open source (MIT). You pay for Claude — Claude.ai has a free tier and a $20/mo Pro plan that covers most organizers. Heavy users on Claude Code may use more, but you'll see usage as you go. There's no contract with us.
What if our committee is not a tech team?
That's the most common case. Use the Claude.ai browser path. The Sponsorship lead doesn't need to know how a CLI works — they just need a project with the Sponsorship skill loaded, and they're a senior partnerships exec on Monday morning.
Can multiple people on our committee use the same skills?
Yes. Each committee member can load whichever skill matches their role into their own Claude account. The skills don't share state across accounts — but they also don't need to. Each person is having a focused conversation with their own AI counterpart.
What's the catch?
There isn't one. We build AI tools for events and hospitality at msg2ai.xyz, and we open-sourced the skills because we wanted every conference team — not just the ones who can hire — to have a real back office. If you end up using AI Ambassador for attendee concierge or ActionNotes for session capture on show day, that's a paid product and how we make money. Everything else is free.
